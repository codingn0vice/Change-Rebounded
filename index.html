Why Change Rebounded exists

The meeting ends, you walk out feeling good. You noticed the problem early enough, it’ll get fixed now.

The meeting ends, you walk out feeling like nothing is moving. Five meetings on the same problem. Five meetings where the topic talked about was something else tangentially related at best. The meetings start feeling like meetings for meeting’s sake. You feel stuck.

It’s a familiar pattern we’ve all seen play out. It happens in almost every organisation. Sometimes just certain teams, sometimes multiple divisions, or even the whole organisation. In every case it costs money. In some it costs opportunities. Some become a statistic under ‘businesses closed this year.’

Change Rebounded exists to help you find your ability to change, adapt and grow again. We help you get unstuck so you can put your energy into getting things done.

The way we work

The last thing a fish is likely to notice is the water it’s swimming in. No consultant can ever know your business like you do. Boundaries like assumptions, biases, and the stories you tell are a reflection of that. But like the fish, you might not notice them.

We work with you find the boundaries that are helping, and the boundaries that might not be. Think of a backyard with a beautiful hardwood fence all around. One part of the backyard has gotten scrappy and no matter what you do it stays the same. You call a landscaper but they can’t get their equipment in. Your fence looks great but it’s stopping you from fixing your backyard. You could take it down, it should only be a couple days.

Then the landscaper finds problems with the pipes in that spot. Now it’s a couple weeks of work. The fence is down the whole time. It’s hard to enjoy your backyard when everyone on the street can see all the way in. The work finishes, the fence goes back up. A month later another spot is dying, you need the landscaper back and that means the fence needs to come down again. So you don’t call. You rather just let the yard go a bit than have the fence down again.

But what if you had a gate? You could do more yourself. Maybe plants trees or create a new garden bed. This is the good enough boundary. It let’s you tend to your organisation and keep it healthy and safe.
